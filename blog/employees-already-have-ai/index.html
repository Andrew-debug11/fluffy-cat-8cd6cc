Your Employees Already Have AI. They Just Don't Know How to Use It.

Published April 27, 2026  •  Peninsulas AI  •  4 min read

Your company probably has AI subscriptions. ChatGPT. Microsoft Copilot. Google Gemini. Maybe all three. Somebody in leadership bought the licenses six months ago because the headlines said AI was going to change everything.

And it did change something. Your team now uses a $20/month tool to rewrite emails they could've sent in two minutes.

That's not a dig. That's what happens when you hand people a power tool with no training. They use it like a screwdriver. Tap a few things. Get frustrated. Go back to doing it the old way.

Meanwhile the subscription bill keeps showing up.

The Real Problem Isn't the Tool

Most businesses don't have an AI problem. They have a training problem.

Your employees aren't dumb. They're busy. Nobody showed them what these tools can actually do for their specific job. Not in a YouTube rabbit hole kind of way. In a "here's how you use this for the report you build every Thursday" kind of way.

There's a gap between having access to AI and knowing what to do with it. That gap is costing you hours every week. Real hours. Across every employee who has a login they barely touch.

What We Actually Teach

Peninsulas AI runs in-person AI training sessions for businesses in Metro Detroit. Two hours. No slides full of buzzwords. No "history of artificial intelligence" lecture.

Here's what a session looks like.

We start with what your team actually does. The spreadsheets they build. The emails they send. The reports they write. The customer questions they answer. Then we show them how to use the AI tools they already pay for to do those things faster.

Not hypothetically. On their laptops. With their files. During the session.

By the end of two hours, every person in the room has at least three workflows they can start using Monday morning. Not "AI concepts." Actual prompts saved to their desktops. Actual templates loaded into their tools. Not AI slop, but quality.

Who This Is For

This isn't for tech companies. It's for the businesses where AI showed up on the credit card statement but never made it into the daily workflow.

Accounting firms where the staff still manually formats every client letter. Construction offices where the project coordinator copies and pastes the same safety briefing into 40 emails. Medical practices where front desk staff spends an hour a day writing appointment confirmations that could be templated in seconds.

If your employees sit at computers and do repetitive work, there's probably $500 to $5,000 a month in wasted time sitting right there. Not from buying new software. From actually using what you've got.

Why In-Person Matters

You can send your team a link to a webinar. You know what happens next. They open it in a tab. They mute it. They do something else. Two weeks later they can't remember what it was about.

In-person training sticks because people do the work during the session. They ask questions specific to their job. They see their coworker figure something out and think "wait, I need that too."

That's how adults learn. Not by watching. By doing. With someone in the room who can look at their screen and say "no, try it this way."

Say you have 10 employees. Each one wastes 30 minutes a day on tasks AI could handle. That's 5 hours a day. 25 hours a week. Over 100 hours a month.

At an average loaded cost of $30/hour, that's $3,000 a month in time you're already paying for. A two-hour training session costs a fraction of one month's waste. The ROI isn't theoretical. It's arithmetic.
